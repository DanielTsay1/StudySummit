Pomodoro
The Pomodoro Technique is an approach to studying that involves studying in intervals of 25 minutes work sessions seperated by short breaks of 5 minutes. After completing typically four sessions, a longer break of 15-30 minutes is taken. This technique maximizes productivity, concentration and motivation throughout the study session.

Active Recall
Active recall is a study technique where you retrieve information from memory without relying on external aids like notes or textbooks. Typically this is done through flashcards or quizlet. This method encourages deeper engagement with the material and enhances long-term retention of the information being studied.
Spaced Repetition
Spaced repetition is a study technique involving reviewing information in spaced intervals over time. This method leverages the spacing effect, which suggests that information is better retained when repeated over spaced intervals rather than in a single session. As a result, spaced repetition is able to optimize long-term memory retention while minimizing the time needed for review.


Feynman Technique
The Feynman Technique is a learning method centered around the concept of teaching a subject to someone else. By teaching the material you are forced to confront any gaps in understanding. The act of explaining the topic helps solidify one's own understanding and retention of the material.
Takeaways and conclusion
While there are many ways and strategies to approach studying, in the end you have to find what works for you. It may involve testing many techniques or creating your own, but find a technique that works for you and that you can stick to. A universal tip for all students taking tests is to take practice tests at least a week before the actual test. The law of specificity says that if you want to get better at something, you need to practice that exact thing. This means taking timed practice tests if that is what you are practicing for. Especially AP students, practice tests will carry you to your desired score. Armed with this information, we hope you find this website useful! Happy studying!!
